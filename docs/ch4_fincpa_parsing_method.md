Chapter 4 Parsing Method
========================

Purpose
-------
This document explains how the first legal dataset is built from the official PDF:

- source: `data/raw/official/law_fincpa_main_2026-01-02.pdf`
- scope: Chapter 4 only
- chapter title: `제4장 금융상품판매업자등의 영업행위 준수사항`
- end boundary: immediately before `제5장 금융소비자 보호`

Why this scope was chosen
-------------------------
The team decided to begin from a narrow and defensible legal source range rather than
claim a broad dataset built from mixed documents. This makes the dataset:

- official
- source-traceable
- clause-level
- reviewable by humans

What this step does
-------------------
This step only performs:

1. deterministic text extraction from the official PDF
2. deterministic segmentation into Chapter 4 legal units
3. storage of clause-level records

This step does not perform:

- LLM summarization
- automatic legal interpretation
- SIR construction from ads
- rule compilation

Did we use Gemini or any LLM here?
----------------------------------
No.

For this dataset-construction step:

- Gemini API was not used
- no LLM was used to extract text
- no LLM was used to decide clause boundaries

The parser is deterministic and local. It uses:

- `pdftotext` for PDF text extraction
- regex-based segmentation for:
  - chapter start
  - chapter end
  - section boundaries
  - article boundaries
  - paragraph markers such as `①`, `②`, `③`

Why we intentionally avoid LLM parsing here
-------------------------------------------
Later, during judging, we need to be able to explain exactly how the legal dataset was built.

The safest answer is:

- the source text came directly from the official PDF
- the chapter boundary was selected explicitly
- the clause boundaries were segmented deterministically
- every output row can be traced back to the original law file

This is stronger than saying:

- "we asked an LLM to read the law and convert it into JSON"

Output files
------------
The parser produces the following files:

- `data/parsed/ch4_fincpa/law_fincpa_main_ch4_fulltext.txt`
- `data/parsed/ch4_fincpa/law_fincpa_main_ch4_clause_records.jsonl`
- `data/parsed/ch4_fincpa/law_fincpa_main_ch4_parse_report.md`
- `data/parsed/ch4_fincpa/law_fincpa_main_ch4_manifest.json`

Clause record design
--------------------
Each parsed row is one clause-level legal record.

Fields:

- `record_id`
- `source_id`
- `source_title`
- `source_path`
- `chapter_id`
- `chapter_title`
- `section_id`
- `section_title`
- `article_id`
- `article_title`
- `paragraph_id`
- `page_start`
- `raw_text`
- `normalized_text`
- `parse_method`
- `manual_verified`

Important distinction: parsing vs annotation
--------------------------------------------
Parsing means:

- extracting official text
- splitting it into legal units
- storing the results faithfully

Annotation means:

- adding labels such as:
  - advertising
  - disclosure
  - approval control
  - prohibited conduct
  - loan
  - deposit
  - insurance

In this first step, we are doing parsing only.

How this later connects to SIR
------------------------------
The legal dataset and the SIR input are different layers.

Legal layer:

- built from the official law
- clause-level records

Content layer:

- built later from ads or customer-facing drafts
- converted into SIR facts such as:
  - seller identity
  - product name
  - fee signal
  - interest disclosure present/missing
  - approval evidence present/missing

Then the system compares:

- legal obligations from parsed law
against
- SIR facts from content input

Example
-------
From Chapter 4, `제22조` contains advertising-related obligations.

Example clause:

- `제22조 제4항`

Meaning in simple terms:

- loan ads must not omit important conditions such as:
  - interest-rate range
  - calculation basis
  - interest timing
  - costs and fees

Later, if an ad SIR says:

- seller present
- product present
- fee cue present
- interest range not evidenced
- interest timing not evidenced

then the future matcher can connect that SIR state back to the parsed legal clause.

What we can prove later
-----------------------
After this parsing step, we can defend the dataset by saying:

- it came from an official law PDF
- it was limited to an explicitly chosen chapter range
- it was segmented deterministically
- it was not generated by an LLM
- every record can be traced back to article, paragraph, page, and source file
